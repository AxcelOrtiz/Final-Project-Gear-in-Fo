Core PC Components:

CPU (Processor)

The CPU is often seen as the “brain” of the computer. It handles calculations, processes instructions, and keeps software running smoothly. Things like core count, clock speed, and architecture determine how fast and capable it is. Popular brands include AMD Ryzen and Intel Core processors.

GPU (Graphics Card)

The GPU is responsible for rendering visuals. If you play games, design 3D models, use AI tools, or edit videos, having a strong GPU makes a big difference. Some computers use integrated graphics built into the CPU, while others rely on dedicated GPUs such as NVIDIA GeForce or AMD Radeon cards.

Motherboard

The motherboard is the central circuit board that connects every part of the PC. It determines what CPU, memory, and storage you can use, along with expansion options. Common motherboard sizes include ATX, Micro-ATX, and Mini-ITX.

RAM (Memory)

RAM stores temporary data that your computer needs to access quickly. More RAM means smoother multitasking and better performance in demanding programs. Today, DDR4 and DDR5 are the most common memory types found in modern PCs.

Storage (SSD / HDD)

Storage keeps your files, programs, photos, and operating system saved. SSDs are fast and allow quick boot times, while HDDs offer more storage at a lower cost. NVMe SSDs are currently the fastest and most efficient option.

Power Supply (PSU)

The PSU delivers electricity to every component inside your PC. Wattage and efficiency ratings (like 80+ Bronze, Gold, or Platinum) help determine how reliable and power-efficient it is.

Cooling System

To prevent overheating and performance loss, PCs rely on cooling. This can include air coolers, liquid cooling, and case fans. Good cooling improves stability and extends the lifespan of components.

Case (Chassis)

The case holds all computer parts in place and protects them. It also affects airflow, aesthetics, and compatibility with parts. Cases come in many styles, sizes, and layouts depending on user needs.

Operating System

The operating system (OS) is what allows you to interact with your hardware and run software. Common operating systems include Windows, Linux distributions, and macOS (which is exclusive to Apple hardware).
